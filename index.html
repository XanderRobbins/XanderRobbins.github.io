About Me

I’m a University of Florida student majoring in Mathematics, Data Science, and Economics, graduating early in 2027. My interests lie at the intersection of probability, statistics, and finance, with a focus on applying rigorous mathematical ideas to real-world problems. I’ve completed advanced coursework in calculus, linear algebra, data structures, and probability, and I’m currently studying real analysis and abstract algebra.

Outside the classroom, I work with a student-run quantitative hedge fund, where I build and test models using Python and C++. I’m driven by deep problem-solving, independent research, and developing quantitative tools that bridge theory and practice.
